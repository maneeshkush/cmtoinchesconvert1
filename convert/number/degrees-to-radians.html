How to convert degrees to radians

Pi radians are equal to 180 degrees:

π rad = 180°

One degree is equal 0.01745329252 radians:

1° = π/180° = 0.005555556π = 0.01745329252 rad

So The angle α in radians is equal to the angle α in degrees times pi constant divided by [180] degrees.

α(radians) = α(degrees) × π / 180°

or

radians = degrees × π / 180°

Example 1

Convert 35 degrees angle to radians:

α(radians) = α(degrees) × π / 180° = 35° × 3.14159 / 180° = 0.6108652382 rad

Example 2

Convert 90 degrees angle to radians:

α(radians) = α(degrees) × π / 180° = 90° × 3.14159 / 180° = 1.5707963268 rad

Example 3

Convert 45 degrees angle to radians:

α(radians) = α(degrees) × π / 180° = 45° × 3.14159 / 180° = 0.7853981634 rad

Example 4

Convert 60 degrees angle to radians:

α(radians) = α(degrees) × π / 180° = 60° × 3.14159 / 180° = 1.0471975512 rad
