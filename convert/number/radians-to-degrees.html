How to convert radians to degrees

Pi radians are equal to 180 degrees:

π rad = 180°

One radian is equal 57.295779513 degrees:

1 rad = 180°/π = 57.295779513°

So The angle α in degrees is equal to the angle α in radians times [180] degrees divided by pi constant.

α(degrees) = α(radians) × 180° / π

or

degrees = radians × 180° / π

Example 1

Convert 4 radians angle to degrees:

α(degrees) = α(radians) × 180° / π = 4×180°/π = 229.18311805°

Example 2

Convert 3 radians angle to degrees:

3 rad = (3 × 57.29578) = 171.88734°

Example 3

Convert 6 radians angle to degrees:

α(degrees) = α(radians) × 180° / π = 6×180°/π = 343.77467708°

Example 4

Convert 7 radians angle to degrees:

α(degrees) = α(radians) × 180° / π = 7×180°/π = 401.07045659°

Example 5

Convert 9 radians angle to degrees:

α(degrees) = α(radians) × 180° / π = 9×180°/π = 515.66201562°
