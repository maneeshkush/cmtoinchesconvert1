How to convert Rankine to Kelvin

So The temperature T in Kelvin (K) is equal to the temperature T in Rankine (°R) times [5/9].

T(K) = T(°R) × 5/9

Example 1

Convert 200 degrees Rankine to Kelvin:

T(K) = 200°R × 5/9 = 111.111 K

Example 2

Convert 320 degrees Rankine to Kelvin:

T(K) = 320°R × 5/9 = 177.77777778 K

Example 3

Convert 350 degrees Rankine to Kelvin:

T(K) = 350°R × 5/9 = 194.44444444 K

Example 4

Convert 400 degrees Rankine to Kelvin:

T(K) = 400°R × 5/9 = 222.22222222 K

Example 5

Convert 500 degrees Rankine to Kelvin:

T(K) = 500°R × 5/9 = 277.77777778 K
