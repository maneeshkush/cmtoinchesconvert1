How to Convert Fahrenheit to Kelvin

So The temperature T in Kelvin (K) is equal to the temperature T in degrees Fahrenheit (°F) plus 459.67, times [5/9].

T(K) = (T(°F) + 459.67)× 5/9

Example 1

Convert 50 degrees Fahrenheit to degrees Kelvin:

T(K) = (50°F + 459.67)× 5/9 = 283.15 K

Example 2

Convert 70 degrees Fahrenheit to degrees Kelvin:

T(K) = (70°F + 459.67)× 5/9 = 294.26111111 K

Example 3

Convert 100 degrees Fahrenheit to degrees Kelvin:

T(K) = (100°F + 459.67)× 5/9 = 310.92777778 K

Example 4

Convert 600 degrees Fahrenheit to degrees Kelvin:

T(K) = (600°F + 459.67)× 5/9 = 588.70555556 K
