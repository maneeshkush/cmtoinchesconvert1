What are ppm and mg/l?

PPM and mg/L are two different measures of substance concentration.

PPM, or parts per million, is the number of parts of a substance in one million parts of a solution or mixture. For example, let's say you want to measure the salinity of water. PPM is the number of parts of salt per million parts of a complete solution of both water and salt.


Mg/L, or milligrams per liter, is a measure of concentration. It tells how many milligrams of the substance can be found in one liter of solution or mixture.

PPM and mg/L . convert between

The relationship between PPM and mg/L depends on the solute density. Imagine that you add 10 grams of the substance to a liter of water. If the substance is as dense as oil, it will have less volume - and as a result, the ppm ratio of the solution will be smaller. For low-density substances (eg alcohol), the ppm ratio will be much higher, even if the mg/L ratio remains constant.

To convert PPM to mg/L, follow these steps:

1. Choose the solute - is the substance diluted with water, acetone or something else? Let's choose an oil with a density equal to 920 kg/m.

2. Set the ppm value for your solution. Let's say you made a solution with 1,230 ppm of oil.

3. Use the following formula to find the mg/L ratio:

milligrams per liter = PPM * density / 1,000

4. In this case,

milligrams per liter = 1,230 * 920 / 1,000 = 1,131.6 mg/L

This means that 1,230 ppm is the equivalent of 1,131.6 mg/l of oil in water.

Special case: water

As you must be aware that the density of water is equal to 1,000 kg/m. This means that every cubic meter of water weighs 1,000 kg. Let's recalculate the units:

1,000 kg/m³
= 1,000,000 g/m³
= 1,000,000,000 mg/m³
= 1,000,000 mg/dm³
= 1,000,000 mg/L

This means that every liter of water contains exactly one million milligrams of water. This means that if the density of the dissolved substance in water is equal to or nearly equal to the density of water, you can assume that 1 ppm = 1 mg/L.

Remember that this analogy is only valid for very specific conditions - pure water at a standard pressure and temperature.

molar concentration calculation

If you already know the mg/L ratio of your solution, you can also use this PPM to mg/L converter to calculate the molarity. This parameter describes the number of moles in a liter of solution and is expressed in molars (1 M = mol/L).

To find the molarity, you need to know an additional parameter - the molar mass of your solute (the amount of water dissolved in it). It is expressed in grams per mole. Use the following formula:

molarity = milligrams per liter / (molar mass * 1,000)

For example, the molar mass of oil is equal to 900 g/mol. We can use the previously calculated mg/L ration to determine the molarity:

molarity = 1,131.6 / (900 * 1,000) = 0.00126 M

What is ppm and how is it used?

PPM stands for "parts per million," and it is a unit of concentration often used in chemistry and environmental science. It refers to the number of parts of a particular substance per million parts of a solution or mixture. PPM is often used to express the concentration of contaminants or pollutants in water, air, or soil.

What is mg/liter and how is it used?

mg/liter, also known as milligrams per liter, is a unit of concentration used to express the amount of a particular substance in a solution or mixture. It refers to the number of milligrams of a substance present in one liter of solution. Like ppm, mg/liter is often used to express the concentration of contaminants or pollutants in water, air, or soil.

How do I convert ppm to mg/liter?

To convert ppm to mg/liter, you can use the following formula:

mg/liter = (ppm * molecular weight of substance) / 1000

For example, if you wanted to convert a concentration of 50 ppm of a substance with a molecular weight of 100 g/mol, the conversion would be:

mg/liter = (50 ppm * 100 g/mol) / 1000 = 5 mg/liter

Can I use a calculator or online converter tool to perform the conversion?

Yes, there are many online converter tools and calculators that can perform the conversion from ppm to mg/liter for you. Simply input the concentration in ppm and the molecular weight of the substance, and the tool will calculate the equivalent concentration in mg/liter.

Are ppm and mg/liter interchangeable units of concentration?

While ppm and mg/liter can both be used to express concentration, they are not interchangeable units. Ppm is based on a ratio of parts per million, while mg/liter is based on a ratio of milligrams per liter. It is important to use the correct unit of concentration based on the context and the type of measurement being taken.

What is the relationship between ppm and mg/liter?

The relationship between ppm and mg/liter depends on the molecular weight of the substance being measured. The conversion from ppm to mg/liter involves multiplying the concentration in ppm by the molecular weight of the substance and dividing by 1000. The resulting concentration in mg/liter will be higher or lower depending on the molecular weight of the substance.
