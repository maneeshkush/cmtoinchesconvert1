How to convert joules to volts

How to convert energy in joules (J) to electrical voltage in volts (V).

You can calculate volts from joules and coulombs, but you can't convert joules to volts since volt and joule units represent different quantities.

Joules to volts calculation formula

The voltage V in volts (V) is equal to the energy E in joules (J), divided by the charge Q in coulombs (C):

V(V) = E(J) / Q(C)

So

volt = joule / coulomb

or

V = J / C

Example 1

What is the voltage supply of an electrical circuit with energy consumption of 50 joules and charge flow of 4 coulombs?

V = 50J / 4C = 12.5V

Example 2

What is the voltage supply of an electrical circuit with energy consumption of 50 joules and charge flow of 5 coulombs?

V = 50J / 5C = 10V

Example 3

What is the voltage supply of an electrical circuit with energy consumption of 80 joules and charge flow of 4 coulombs?

V = 80J / 4C = 20V

Example 4

What is the voltage supply of an electrical circuit with energy consumption of 100 joules and charge flow of 4 coulombs?

V = 100J / 4C = 25

Example 5

What is the voltage supply of an electrical circuit with energy consumption of 500 joules and charge flow of 4 coulombs?

V = 500J / 4C = 125V

FAQ

Is joules equal to voltage?

Volt is a unit of measurement designating the electric potential or voltage in a conductor. However joule is a unit of energy or work done to move electric charge through electric potential.

How do joules relate to volts?

One joule of energy is defined as the energy expended by one ampere on one volt, which moves in one second.

How do you convert joules to power?

In general, power is defined as energy over time. Watt is defined as 1 Watt = 1 Joule per second (1W = 1 J/s), which means that 1 kW = 1000 J/s.
