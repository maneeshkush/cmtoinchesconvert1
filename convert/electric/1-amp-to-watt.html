How to convert 1 amp to watts

How to convert electric current of 1 amp (A) to electric power in watts (W).

You can calculate (but not convert) the watts from amps and volts:

1A to watts calculation with voltage of 12V DC

In a DC circuit, watts (W) are equal to amps (A) multiplied by volts (V). So, to calculate the power in watts, you can simply multiply the current in amps by the voltage in volts.

For example, if you have a 12V DC power supply and you want to know how many watts it can deliver, you can calculate it like this:

watts = amps × volts

watts = 1A × 12V = 12W

This means that the power supply can deliver 12 watts of power.

It's important to note that this calculation is only valid for DC circuits. In an AC circuit, the relationship between watts, amps, and volts is a bit more complex and depends on the phase angle between the current and voltage.

1A to watts calculation with voltage of 120V AC

In an AC circuit, the relationship between watts, amps, and volts is more complex than in a DC circuit because the current and voltage are not always in phase with each other. The power factor (PF) is a measure of the phase angle between the current and voltage and is used to represent the amount of real power (in watts) being delivered to the load.

For a resistive load (like a heating element), the power factor is typically close to 1, which means that the current and voltage are in phase and the watts can be calculated using the formula you provided:

watts = PF × amps × volts

watts = 1 × 1A × 120V = 120W

For an inductive load (like an induction motor), the power factor is typically less than 1, which means that the current and voltage are out of phase. In this case, the power factor can be used to correct the calculation of watts. For example, if the power factor of an inductive load is approximately 0.8:

watts = PF × amps × volts

watts = 0.8 × 1A × 120V = 96W

It's important to note that the power factor can vary depending on the specific load and the operating conditions, so it's always a good idea to measure or calculate the power factor before making any power calculations.
