What units are BTU?

British thermal units, or BTUs, are a unit of energy. This is the amount of energy required to heat one pound of water by one degree Fahrenheit to one atm.

How do you calculate BTU load?

You may be familiar with the formula BTUH = CFM x ΔT x 1.08. This same formula is often rearranged to be used to determine air flow by measuring heat input and temperature rise.

What is 1 ton equal to in BTU?

The metrics British thermal units per hour (BTUH) and tonnage (tonne) are used to determine the cooling capacity of an air conditioner in an hour. One ton of cooling capacity equals 12,000 BTU.
