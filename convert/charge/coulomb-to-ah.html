How to convert Coulombs to ampere-hours

One Coulomb is the amount of charge that flows past a point in an electric circuit in one second. An ampere-hour (Ah) is a unit of electric charge, it is the amount of charge that flows past a point in an electric circuit in one hour. To convert Coulombs to ampere-hours, multiply the number of Coulombs by the number of hours.

The difference between Coulombs and ampere-hours

is that Coulombs measure the electric charge while ampere-hours measure the electric current. Coulombs are named after French physicist Charles-Augustin de Coulomb, who developed the electrostatic force law in 1785. Ampere-hours are named after French physicist André-Marie Ampère, who developed the Ampère's law in 1826.

Coulombs and ampere-hours are both units of electric charge, but Coulombs measure the total charge while ampere-hours measure the current multiplied by time. For example, if a battery has a current of 1 amp and is left for 10 hours, the battery will have 10 ampere-hours of charge.

This is measured in ampere-hours or watt-hours. The current of the battery is how much energy the battery is using at any given time. This is measured in amps.
