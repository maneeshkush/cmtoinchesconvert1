Electron charge to coulombs conversion formula

The charge in coulombs Q(C) is equal to the charge in electron charge Q(e) times 1.60217646⋅10-19:

Q(C) = Q(e) × 1.60217646⋅10-19

What is meant by 1 coulomb?

Coulomb is the SI unit of electric charge which is equal to the amount of charge transported by a current of one ampere in one second. It can also be a property of a substance that produces electric and magnetic effects. It is denoted by C. Mathematically, 1 coulomb = 1 ampere × 1 second.
