DC kilowatts to volts calculation

So The voltage V in volts (V) is equal to [1000] times the power P in kilowatts (kW), divided by the current  I in amps (A).

V(V) = 1000 × P(kW) / I(A)

Example 1

What is the voltage in volts when the power consumption is 5 kilowatts and the current flow is 3 amps?

V = 5 kW / 3A = 1666.666V

Example 2

What is the voltage in volts when the power consumption is 10 kilowatts and the current flow is 3 amps?

V = 10 kW / 3A = 3333.333V

AC single phase kilowatts to volts calculation

So The voltage V in volts (V) is equal to [1000] times the power P in kilowatts (kW), divided by the power factor  PF times the current I in amps (A).

V(V) = 1000 × P(kW) / (PF × I(A) )

Example 1

What is the RMS voltage in volts when the power consumption is 5 kilowatts, the power factor is 0.8 and the phase current is 3.75 amps?

V = 1000 × 5kW / (0.8 × 3.75A) = 1666.666V

Example 2

What is the RMS voltage in volts when the power consumption is 10 kilowatts, the power factor is 0.8 and the phase current is 3.85 amps?

V = 1000 × 10kW / (0.8 × 3.85A) = 3246.753V

AC three phase kilowatts to volts calculation

So The line to line RMS voltage VL-L in volts (V) is equal to [1000] times the power P in kilowatts (kW), divided by square root of 3 times the power factor  PF times the current  I in amps (A).

VL-L(V) = 1000 × P(kW) / (√3 × PF × I(A) )

         ≈ 1000 × P(kW) / (1.732 × PF × I(A) )

Example 1

What is the RMS voltage in volts when the power consumption is 5 kilowatts, the power factor is 0.8 and the phase current flow is 2.165 amps?

V = 1000 × 5kW / (√3 × 0.8 × 2.165A) = 1666V

Example 2

What is the RMS voltage in volts when the power consumption is 10 kilowatts, the power factor is 0.8 and the phase current flow is 2.165 amps?

V = 1000 × 10kW / (√3 × 0.8 × 2.165A) = 3333V
