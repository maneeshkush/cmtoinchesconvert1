Watts to VA calculation

So The apparent power S in volt-amps (VA) is equal to the real power P in watts (W), divided by the power factor PF.

S(VA) =  P(W) / PF

Example 1

What is the apparent power in volt-amps when the real power is 2000W and the power factor is 0.8?

Solution:

S = 2000W / 0.8 = 2500VA

Example 2

What is the apparent power in volt-amps when the real power is 4000W and the power factor is 0.8?

Solution:

S = 4000W / 0.8 = 5000VA

Example 3

What is the apparent power in volt-amps when the real power is 5000W and the power factor is 0.8?

Solution:

S = 5000W / 0.8 = 6250VA

Example 4

What is the apparent power in volt-amps when the real power is 8000W and the power factor is 0.8?

Solution:

S = 8000W / 0.8 = 10000VA
