DC watts to amps calculation

So The current I in amps (A) is equal to the power  P in watts (W), divided by the voltage  V in volts (V).

I(A) = P(W) / V(V)

Example 1

What is the current in amps when the power consumption is 330 watts and the voltage supply is 120 volts?

I = 330W / 120V = 2.75A

Example 2

What is the current in amps when the power consumption is 330 watts and the voltage supply is 140 volts?

I = 330W / 140V = 2.35A

AC single phase watts to amps calculation

So The phase current I in amps (A) is equal to the power  P in watts (W), divided by the power factor  PF times the RMS voltage  V in volts (V).

I(A) = P(W) / (PF × V(V))

So The power factor of resistive impedance load is equal to [1].

Example 1

What is the phase current in amps when the power consumption is 330 watts, the power factor is 0.8 and the RMS voltage supply is 120 volts?

I = 330W / (0.8 × 120V) = 3.4375A

Example 2

What is the phase current in amps when the power consumption is 330 watts, the power factor is 0.8 and the RMS voltage supply is 150 volts?

I = 330W / (0.8 × 150V) = 2.75A

AC three phase watts to amps calculation

Calculation with line to line voltage

So The phase current I in amps (A) is equal to the power  P in watts (W), divided by square root of 3 times the power factor  PF times the line to line RMS voltage  VL-L in volts (V).

I(A) = P(W) / (√3 × PF × VL-L(V) )

So The power factor of resistive impedance load is equal to [1].

Example 1

What is the phase current in amps when the power consumption is 330 watts, the power factor is 0.8 and the RMS voltage supply is 120 volts?

I = 330W / (√3 × 0.8 × 120V) = 1.984A

Example 2

What is the phase current in amps when the power consumption is 330 watts, the power factor is 0.8 and the RMS voltage supply is 140 volts?

I = 330W / (√3 × 0.8 × 140V) = 1.701A

Calculation with line to neutral voltage

So The phase current I in amps (A) is equal to the power  P in watts (W), divided by 3 times the power factor  PF times the line to neutral RMS voltage  VL-N in volts (V).

I(A) = P(W) / (3 × PF × VL-N(V) )

So The power factor of resistive impedance load is equal to [1].
