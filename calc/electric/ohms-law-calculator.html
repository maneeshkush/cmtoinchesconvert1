Ohm's law calculation formula

The current I in amps (A) is equal to the voltage V in volts (V) divided by the resistance R in ohms (Ω):

I = V / R

Example 1

I = 30V / 15Ω = 2A

Example 2

I = 40V / 20Ω = 2A

So The power P in watts (W) is equal to the voltage V in volts (V) times the current I in amps (A).

P = V × I

Example 1

P = 30V × 2A = 60W

Example 2

P = 40V × 2A = 80W
