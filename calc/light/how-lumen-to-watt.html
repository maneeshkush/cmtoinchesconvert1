How to convert lumens to watts

How to convert luminous flux in lumens (lm) to electric power in watts (W).

You can calculate watts from lumens and luminous efficacy.  Lumen and watt units represent different quantities, so you can't convert lumens to watts.

Lumens to watts calculation formula

So The power P in watts (W) is equal to the luminous flux ΦV in lumens (lm), divided by the luminous efficacy η in lumens per watt (lm/W).

P(W) = ΦV(lm) / η(lm/W)

So

watts = lumens / (lumens per watt)

or

W = lm / (lm/W)

Example 1

What is the power consumption of a lamp that has luminous flux of 900 lumens and luminous efficacy of 25 lumens per watt (lm/W)?

P = 900 lm / 25 lm/W = 36 W

Example 2

What is the power consumption of a lamp that has luminous flux of 900 lumens and luminous efficacy of 35 lumens per watt (lm/W)?

P = 900 lm / 35 lm/W = 25.71 W

Example 3

What is the power consumption of a lamp that has luminous flux of 1200 lumens and luminous efficacy of 15 lumens per watt (lm/W)?

P = 1200 lm / 15 lm/W = 80 W

Example 4

What is the power consumption of a lamp that has luminous flux of 1500 lumens and luminous efficacy of 15 lumens per watt (lm/W)?

P = 1500 lm / 15 lm/W = 100 W

Example 5

What is the power consumption of a lamp that has luminous flux of 1700 lumens and luminous efficacy of 15 lumens per watt (lm/W)?

P = 1700 lm / 15 lm/W = 113.33 W
